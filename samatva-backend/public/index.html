Day 12 of Reading ☸
☸ Bhagavad Gita · Chapter 2, Verse 48
संस्कृत (Sanskrit)
योगस्थः कुरु कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा धनञ्जय।
सिद्ध्यसिद्ध्योः समो भूत्वा समत्वं योग उच्यते॥
✦
Roman Transliteration
"Yogasthaḥ kuru karmāṇi saṅgaṁ tyaktvā Dhanañjaya,
Siddhyasiddhyoḥ samo bhūtvā samatvaṁ yoga ucyate."
✦
हिंदी अनुवाद (Hindi)
हे धनञ्जय! योग में स्थित होकर कर्म कर, आसक्ति त्याग दे और सिद्धि-असिद्धि में समान भाव रख — इसी समभाव को योग कहते हैं।
✦
O Arjuna, perform your actions established in Yoga, abandoning attachment, and remain even-minded in success and failure. This evenness of mind is called Yoga — Samatva.
✦ Today's Reflection from Antara
This verse is the very heart of Samatva — equanimity. Krishna is not asking Arjuna to be emotionless, but to act with full dedication while releasing the anxiety over outcomes. In your life today, where are you over-attached to a specific result? Can you act wholeheartedly and trust the process?
💡 How to Apply This Today
Before a difficult conversation or decision, pause and ask: "Am I acting from duty or from fear of outcome?" If it's fear, breathe. Return to your values. Then act. The Gita suggests that the quality of your inner state shapes the quality of your action — not the other way around.
